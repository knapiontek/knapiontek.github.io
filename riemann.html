Polar Coordinates in Euclidan Space

\vec{V} = V^a e_a = V^{b} e_{b} \frac{\partial\vec{V}}{\partial x^b} = \frac{\partial{V^a}}{\partial x^b}e_a + V^a\frac{\partial{e_a}}{\partial x^b} \begin{cases} x = r\cos\phi\\ y = r\sin\phi \end{cases} e_a = \frac{\partial{x_a}}{\partial x^b}e_b \begin{bmatrix} e_r \\ e_\phi \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial r} \\ \\ \frac{\partial x}{\partial\phi} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial\phi} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}e_x \\ e_y\end{bmatrix} \begin{cases} e_r = \cos\phi e_x + \sin\phi e_y \\ e_\phi = -r\sin\phi e_x + r\cos\phi e_y \end{cases} \begin{cases} \frac{\partial e_r}{\partial r} = 0 \\ \\ \frac{\partial e_r}{\partial\phi} = -\sin\phi e_x + \cos\phi e_y = \frac{1}{r}e_\phi \\ \\ \frac{\partial e_\phi}{\partial r} = -\sin\phi e_x + \cos\phi e_y = \frac{1}{r}e_\phi \\ \\ \frac{\partial e_\phi}{\partial\phi} = -r\cos\phi e_x - r\sin\phi e_y = -e_r \end{cases}
