Poisson Equation

\nabla^2\vec{u} = f

1-Dimention

\frac{d^2u}{dx} = f(x)

Taylor series approximation

\begin{aligned} u(x-h)&=u(x)-hu'(x)+\frac{1}{2}h^2u''(x)-\mathcal{O}(h^3) \\ \\ u(x+h)&=u(x)+hu'(x)+\frac{1}{2}h^2u''(x)+\mathcal{O}(h^3) \\ \\ u(x-h)+u(x+h)&=2u(x)+h^2u''(x) \\ \\ u''(x)&=\frac{u(x-h)-2u(x)+u(x+h)}{h^2} \end{aligned}

Approximation by definition

\begin{aligned} u_-'(x)&=\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{u(x)-u(x-h)}{h} \\ \\ u_+'(x)&=\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{u(x+h)-u(x)}{h} \\ \\ u''(x)&=\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{u_+'(x)-u_-'(x)}{h} \\ \\ &=\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{u(x-h)-2u(x)+u(x+h)}{h^2} \end{aligned}

Euler method algorithm where u_n''=f_n

u_{n-1}-2u_n+u_{n+1}=h^2f_n

Matrix equation example for 5-steps with boundraries u_0 \to c_0, u_6 \to c_6 and uniform h

\begin{bmatrix} -2 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \\ u_5 \end{bmatrix} =h^2 \begin{bmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \\ f_3 \\ f_4 \\ f_5 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} c_0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ c_6 \end{bmatrix}

Integration by parts

\int \frac{du}{dx}v = uv-\int u\frac{dv}{dx}

Numerical Example

\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = e^{-x^2}(4x^2 - 2)

First integral

c_1 = 0 \begin{cases} u=e^{-x^2} \to \frac{du}{dx} = -2xe^{-x^2} \to du=-2xe^{-x^2}dx \\ v=-2x \end{cases} \begin{aligned} \frac{dy}{dx} &= \int e^{-x^2}(4x^2-2)dx \\ &= \int 4x^2e^{-x^2} - \int 2e^{-x^2}dx \\ &= -2xe^{-x^2} + \int 2e^{-x^2}dx - \int 2e^{-x^2}dx \\ &= -2xe^{-x^2} \end{aligned}

Second integral

c_2 = 0 u=-x^2 \to du=-2xdx \begin{aligned} y &= \int -2xe^{-x^2}dx \\ &= e^{-x^2} \\ \end{aligned}

2-Dimentions

\frac{\partial^2u}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial^2u}{\partial y} = f(x,y)

Taylor series approximation as in 1-Dimention

\begin{aligned} u''(x)&=\frac{u(x-h)-2u(x)+u(x+h)}{h^2} \\ \\ u''(y)&=\frac{u(y-h)-2u(y)+u(y+h)}{h^2} \\ \\ u''(x,y)&=u''(x)+u''(y) \\ \\ &=\frac{u(x-h,y)+u(x,y-h)-4u(x,y)+u(x+h,y)+u(x,y+h)}{h^2} \end{aligned}

Approximation by definition

\begin{aligned} u_-'(x,y)&=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{u(x,y)-u(x-h,y)}{h}+\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{u(x,y)-u(x,y-h)}{h} \\ \\ u_+'(x,y)&=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{u(x+h,y)-u(x,y)}{h}+\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{u(x,y+h)-u(x,y)}{h} \\ \\ u''(x,y)&=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{u_+'(x,y)-u_-'(x,y)}{h} \\ \\ &=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{u(x-h,y)+u(x,y-h)-4u(x,y)+u(x+h,y)+u(x,y+h)}{h^2} \end{aligned}

Euler method algorithm where u_{x,y}''=f_{x,y}

u_{x-1,y}+u_{x,y-1}-4u_{x,y}+u_{x+1,y}+u_{x,y+1}=h^2f_{x,y}
