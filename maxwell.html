Integral Maxwell Equations

\begin{aligned} \oint_{\partial V} B \quad & = \quad 0 & \quad \text{(Gauss)} \\ \nabla\cdot E \quad & = \quad 0 & \quad \text{(Colomb)} \\ \frac{\partial E}{\partial t} \quad & = \quad \nabla\times B & \quad \text{(Faraday)} \\ \frac{\partial B}{\partial t} \quad & = \quad -\nabla\times E & \quad \text{(Ampere)} \end{aligned}

Differencial Maxwell Equations

\begin{aligned} \nabla\cdot B \quad & = \quad 0 & \quad \text{(Gauss)} \\ \nabla\cdot E \quad & = \quad \frac{\rho}{\epsilon} & \quad \text{(Colomb)} \\ \\ \nabla\times B \quad & = \quad \frac{\partial E}{\partial t} & \quad \text{(Faraday)} \\ \\ \nabla\times E \quad & = \quad -\frac{\partial B}{\partial t} & \quad \text{(Ampere)} \end{aligned}
