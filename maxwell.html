Integral Maxwell Equations

\begin{aligned} 1.\quad & \oint_{\partial\Omega}\vec{E}\cdot d\vec{a} = 4\pi\int_{\Omega}\rho dV \\ \\ 2.\quad & \oint_{\partial\Omega}\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{a} = 0 \\ \\ 3.\quad & \oint_{\partial\Sigma}\vec{E}\cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{1}{c}\frac{d}{dt}\int_\Sigma\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{a} \\ \\ 4.\quad & \oint_{\partial\Sigma}\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l} = \frac{1}{c}\Bigl(4\pi\int_\Sigma\vec{J}\cdot\vec{a}+\frac{d}{dt}\int_\Sigma\vec{E}\cdot d\vec{a}\Bigr) \\ \end{aligned}

Differencial Maxwell Equations

\begin{aligned} 1.\quad & \nabla\cdot\vec{E} = 4\pi\rho \\ \\ 2.\quad & \nabla\cdot\vec{B} = 0 \\ \\ 3.\quad & \nabla\times\vec{E} = -\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\vec{B}}{\partial t} \\ \\ 4.\quad & \nabla\times\vec{B} = \frac{1}{c}\Bigl(4\pi\vec{J}+\frac{\partial\vec{E}}{\partial t}\Bigr) \\ \end{aligned}
  1. The electric flux leaving a volume is proportional to the charge inside.
  2. There are no magnetic monopoles; the total magnetic flux through a closed surface is zero.
  3. The voltage induced in a closed circuit is proportional to the rate of change of the magnetic flux it encloses.
  4. The magnetic field integrated around a closed loop is proportional to the electric current plus displacement current (rate of change of electric field) it encloses.
