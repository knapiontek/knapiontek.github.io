Consider 2 cubic polynomial equations

\begin{cases} x_0(t) = a_0t^3 + b_0t^2 + c_0t + d_0 \\ x'_0(t) = 3a_0t^2 + 2b_0t + c_0 \\ x''_0(t) = 6a_0t + 2b_0 \end{cases} \begin{cases} x_1(t) = a_1t^3 + b_1t^2 + c_1t + d_1 \\ x'_1(t) = 3a_1t^2 + 2b_1t + c_1 \\ x''_1(t) = 6a_1t + 2b_1 \end{cases}

Let's evaluated them in points t=0, t=1

\begin{cases} x_0(0) = d_0 \\ x'_0(0) = c_0 \\ x''_0(0) = 2b_0 \\ x_0(1) = a_0 + b_0 + c_0 + d_0 \\ x'_0(1) = 3a_0 + 2b_0 + c_0 \\ x''_0(1) = 6a_0 + 2b_0 \end{cases} \begin{cases} x_1(0) = d_1 \\ x'_1(0) = c_1 \\ x''_1(0) = 2b_1 \\ x_1(1) = a_1 + b_1 + c_1 + d_1 \\ x'_1(1) = 3a_1 + 2b_1 + c_1 \\ x''_1(1) = 6a_1 + 2b_1 \end{cases}

We add extra constraints for boundaries and to ensure continuity in the point of joining

\begin{cases} x'_0(0) = 0 \\ x'_0(1) = x'_1(0) \\ x''_0(1) = x''_1(0) \\ x'_1(1) = 0 \end{cases}

We can expand it for 3 fixed points x_0, x_1, x_2

\begin{cases} x_0 = d_0 \\ x_1 = a_0 + b_0 + c_0 + d_0 \\ x_1 = d_1 \\ x_2 = a_1 + b_1 + c_1 + d_1 \\ c_0 = 0 \\ 3a_0 + 2b_0 + c_0 = c_1 \\ 6a_0 + 2b_0 = 2b_1 \\ 3a_1 + 2b_1 + c_1 = 0 \end{cases}

Simplifying

\begin{cases} a_0 + b_0 = x_1 - x_0 \\ a_1 + b_1 + c_1 = -x_1 - x_2 \\ 3a_0 + 2b_0 + 0 - c_1 = 0 \\ 6a_0 + 2b_0 - 2b_1 = 0 \\ 3a_1 + 2b_1 + c_1 = 0 \end{cases}

In the matrix notation

\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 & 0 & 0 & -1 \\ 6 & 2 & 0 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_0 \\ b_0 \\ a_1 \\ b_1 \\ c_1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 - x_0 \\ -x_1 - x_2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}
