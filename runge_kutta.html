Runge-Kutta First Order

y' = f(y, t)

Taylor Series

\begin{matrix} y_{n+1} = y_n + \Delta t y' + \mathcal{O}(\Delta t^2) \\ \end{matrix}

One step error is \mathcal{O}(\Delta t^2) and total error is \mathcal{O}(\Delta t)

\begin{matrix} y_{n+1} = y_n + \Delta t y' \\ y_{n+1} = y_n + \Delta t f(y, t) \end{matrix}

Numeric example

y'' + 2y' + 3y = 2\cos(\omega t-\frac{\pi}{3})

Expected Stationary Solution

y = a \cos\omega t + b\sin\omega t

Let's search for coefficients by substituting expected solution in the example

\begin{matrix} (a\cos\omega t + b\sin\omega t)'' + 2(a\cos\omega t + b\sin\omega t)' + 3(a\cos\omega t + b\sin\omega t) = 2\cos(\omega t - \frac{\pi}{3}) \\ (a\cos\omega t + b\sin\omega t)'' + (2a\cos\omega t + 2b\sin\omega t)' + 3a\cos\omega t + 3b\sin\omega t = 2\cos\frac{\pi}{3}\cos\omega t + 2\sin\frac{\pi}{3}\sin\omega t \\ (-a\omega\sin\omega t + b\omega\cos\omega t)' - 2a\omega\sin\omega t + 2b\omega\cos\omega t + 3a\cos\omega t + 3b\sin\omega t = \cos\omega t + \sqrt{3}\sin\omega t \\ -a\omega^2\cos\omega t - b\omega^2\sin\omega t - 2a\omega\sin\omega t + 2b\omega\cos\omega t + 3a\cos\omega t + 3b\sin\omega t = \cos\omega t + \sqrt{3}\sin\omega t \end{matrix}
\begin{cases} -a\omega^2 + 2b\omega + 3a = 1 \\ -b\omega^2 - 2a\omega + 3b = \sqrt{3} \end{cases}
\begin{cases} -4a + 4b + 3a = 1 \\ -4b - 4a + 3b = \sqrt{3} \end{cases}
\begin{cases} -a + 4b = 1 \\ -b - 4a = \sqrt{3} \end{cases}
\begin{cases} a = 4b - 1 \\ -b - 4(4b-1) = \sqrt{3} \end{cases}
\begin{cases} a = 4b - 1 \\ -b - 16b - 4 = \sqrt{3} \end{cases}
\begin{cases} a = 4b - 1 \\ b = \frac{\sqrt{3} + 4}{-17} \end{cases}

Adding by sides we get

-(a+b)\omega^2 - 2(a-b)\omega + 3(a+b) = 1 + \sqrt{3}

Function is at extrimum when derivative equals zero

\begin{matrix} -2(a+b)\omega - 2(a-b) = 0 \\ \omega_0 = \frac{2(a-b)}{-2(a+b)} \\ \omega_0 = \frac{b-a}{a+b} \\ \end{matrix}

Fixing a=1,~b=2 we find resonance at

\omega_0 = \frac{1}{3}

Actual solution

\begin{matrix} y = \cos\frac{1}{3}t + 2\sin\frac{1}{3}t \\ \\ y(0) = 1 \\ \\ y'(0) = -\frac{1}{3}\sin\frac{1}{3}t + \frac{2}{3}\cos\frac{1}{3}t = \frac{2}{3} \end{matrix}

Lets compare analytical solution with numerical method

Order reduction

\begin{cases} y' = x \\ x' + 2x + 3y = 2\cos(\frac{1}{3}t-\frac{\pi}{3}) \end{cases}

Recursive algorithm

\begin{cases} y_{n+1} = y_n + x_n \Delta t \\ x_{n+1} = x_n - 2x_n \Delta t - 3y_{n+1} \Delta t + 2\cos(\frac{1}{3}t-\frac{\pi}{3})\Delta t \end{cases}
