Let take two inercial system frames

\begin{matrix}S_1 & S_2\end{matrix}

where relative velocities are described as follows

\begin{matrix}v_1 = -v_2 & v_2 = -v_1\end{matrix}

Classic theory

\begin{matrix}x_1 = x_2 - vt & x_2 = x_1 + vt\end{matrix}

does not follow observation that speed of light is constant in all directions and in all systems

\begin{matrix}x_1 = ct_1 & x_2 = ct_2\end{matrix}

Let's find new relation beetwen coordinates of these systems

\begin{matrix}x_1 = ax_2 + bt_2 & x_2 = ax_1 + bt_1\end{matrix}

where a and b are new coefficients we need to find

When S_1 and S_2 are at the same location at the begging

\begin{matrix}0 = ax_2 + bt_2 & 0 = ax_1 + bt_1\end{matrix}

then

\begin{matrix}b = a\frac{x_2}{t_2} = -av & b = a\frac{x_1}{t_1} = av\end{matrix}
\begin{matrix}x_1 = ax_2 - avt_2 & x_2 = ax_1 + avt_1\end{matrix}
\begin{matrix}x_1 = a(x_2 - vt_2) & x_2 = a(x_1 + vt_1)\end{matrix}

We postulate that the speed of the light is constant and the same in any system

\begin{matrix}ct_1 = a(ct_2 - vt_2) & ct_2 = a(ct_1 + vt_1)\end{matrix}
\begin{matrix}ct_1 = at_2(c - v) & ct_2 = at_1(c + v)\end{matrix}

Let's multiply it by sides

c^2t_1t_2 = a^2t_1t_2(c^2 - v^2)
c^2 = a^2(c^2 - v^2)
a^2 = \frac{c^2}{c^2 - v^2} = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}
a = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}

Finally Lorentz Transformation

\begin{matrix}x_1 = \frac{x_2 - vt_2}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} & x_2 = \frac{x_1 + vt_1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}\end{matrix}
